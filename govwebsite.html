Education

We will increase funding to public schools in low-income areas. Give the federal government more of a role in funding schools to fairly distribute money. This will raise the bar for public education overall, enhancing future human capital across the United States.

Healthcare

We will gradually switch the United States to the Bismarck Model of healthcare (described in the evidence). Will slowly increase regulation on health insurance while increasing the portion paid for by the government, easing into the change. The plan will be completed by 2034 and will aim to increase the affordability and fairness of the healthcare system for all Americans.

The Environment

We will begin to enforce stricter regulations and make safer work places for those working in oil rigs and fossil fuels. To do this, our plan would be to reopen oil drilling and collection in American, rather than sourcing from foreign countries. With domestic oil rigging, America can ensure that greater regulations and precautions can be taken, and loosen American dependence on foreign production.

Taxes

We will create a new policy where a new, higher, tax bracket is added to the layered tax bracket system. This higher bracket would be for individuals with an annual salary of >$1,000,000 or a household income of >$1,200,000, and it would be taxed a 40% income tax. The new tax revenues would be allocated toward the spending and new infrastructure built through our presidency.

Animal Rights

We will support animal rights by promoting humane, healthy adoption practices. Will require check-ups, six and twelve months after adoption, on families or individuals who have adopted pets; will also provide improved online services for taking care of animals. This will decrease the number of stray animals in the streets and ensure they instead have safe homes.

Government Technology

We will streamline and update government technology systems. Current services, like the federal student aid application, DMV and IRS websites, and others, are outdated and have bugs. Will provide 30% more funding to technology across US departments and encourage development improvements to reduce wasted time, strengthen security, and increase accessibility for all users of technological services.

Immigration Protections

America has been involved in many controversial destabalization of countries, leaving many people of other nations without safety or a home. Our candidate will work to sustain the numbers by the Biden Administration and enhance the immigration system. We aim to ensure that people are being fairly and safely moved into the country.

Electric Vehicles

We will provide a national subsidy for hybrid and electric vehicles to increase their supply across all states, decreasing their price and increasing their equilibrium quantity. This will put more such vehicles on the roads instead of fossil-fuel-reliant vehicles. By reducing pollution and decreasing the detrimental effects of greenhouse gases in climate change, we will have a positive effect on the environment.

Infrastructure

We will improve infrastructure across the country by incentivizing cities to better-maintain roads, highways, tunnels, and bridges. Will provide funding on a national level based on populations and existing conditions to ensure an equal effect. This will increase everyday quality of life and safety while simultaneously creating new jobs.

Public Transportation

We will work to support the upkeep and evironmental advantages of using public transportation. Our goal as an Administration would be to set a mandate that requires all public transportation to be electric by 2040. This would improve the carbon emissions and make public transportation an even safer way for the environment to heal.

Who Is Diego?




Diego DiPasquo is a candidate who can represent and relate to the people of America. Born to a family comprised of a first-generation Mexican mother and an Italian-American father, Diego spent his early years raised in Southcentral Los Angeles. While living in Southcentral, he was shown the harshness of class separation in America, further highlighted by his parents' involvement in social activist groups such as The Labor Community Strategy Center's Bus Riders Union. When Diego was five years old, his father got married to a Kenyan immigrant, which expanded his family to having 3 new African-American siblings, widening his eyes to not just the injustices of class, but also those of racial prejudice. As he got older, DiPasquo's family moved to Redondo Beach, a thriving upper-middle-class suburban neighborhood. The differences in the quality of life between the lower and middle class became all too clear to him as he spent time raised in Redondo Beach. This motivated DiPasquo to stay active in the activist groups he was raised in in LA, and even help to organize multiple Black Lives Matter rallies with his older brothers. As he left high school, DiPasquo knew he wanted to continue making a difference in his community, leading him to go to the University of Nebraska-Lincoln, as Lincoln, Nebraska is one of America's biggest post-Afghanistan immigrant and refugee cities.

After graduating from the University of Nebraska-Lincoln, Diego DiPasquo attended the University of Southern California's Gould School of Law, where he specialized in environmental law and gained inspiration for his first Pulitzer Prize-winning novel. Knowing that not everyone had access to the same education that he did, DiPasquo became motivated to run for local office as a city councilmember of Los Angeles, which would become his first government position. He spent 8 years on the city council advocating for widely-applauded education improvements and environmental protections before being elected as the governor of California. For the past 8 years, he has been increasing funding to public schools, promoting electric vehicles and public transportation, and building new infrastructure across the state. He now hopes to take his policies to the national level as President of the United States of America.
